The true gay agenda revealed!

As our society becomes more focused on equity and inclusion, queer representation has begun to make its way into cartoon content!

The Legend of Korra

2012 - 2014

One of the earliest depictions of a queer relationship, this series shows the main character as bisexual.

Though not expressly shown, her relationship with another main female character is strongly insinuated, and the finale shows the two holding hands.

Steven Universe

2013 - 2015

Steven Universe creator Rebecca Sugar was told by executives that including queer romantic relationships could end her show.

Nevertheless, she moved forward to openly tell the story of several queer characters and the interpersonal relationships they share.

She-Ra and the Princesses of Power

2018 - 2020

She-Ra creator Noelle Stevenson faced similar feedback to Rebecca Sugar, being told the characters couldn't be openly queer.

By the time the final season premiered in 2020, she had been persistent and the relationships that were hinted were finally confirmed.

The Dragon Prince

2018 - ??

Netflix series The Dragon Prince features several queer side characters. There are warrior Queens and Moon Elf husbands who openly kiss on the show.

The queer relationships are not primary plot, but they are treated as normative and the show doesn't shy away from showing their affection.

Kipo and the Age of Wonderbeasts

2020 - ??

Another Netflix show from the same era, one of the three main characters in post-apocalyptic tale "Kipo and the Age of Wonderbeasts" is openly gay.

Troy has yet to have a relationship, but the second season brought in a new character to be the object of his affection.

The Owl House

2020 - ??

Disney finally took a step toward equitable representation in its new series, "The Owl House."

In a prom-themed episode, it is revealed that the witch Amity has a crush on main character Luz, a human spending her summer in a magical realm.
